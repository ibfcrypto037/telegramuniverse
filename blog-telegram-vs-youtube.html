Telegram vs YouTube Monetization: Which Pays More?

By TelegramUniverse Team • 8 min read

The creator economy is shifting, and new challengers are continually threatening the legacy platforms. A debate raging among modern influencers concerns the ultimate telegram channel revenue comparison to YouTube. While YouTube has reigned supreme as the default monetization beast via AdSense for a decade, Telegram’s integration of Toncoin-based native ads has completely flipped the script. So, which is the best platform for content creators looking to actually maximize their income?

Revenue Splits and Mechanics

On YouTube, AdSense currently grants creators 55% of total ad revenue across long-form content and 45% on Shorts. Contrast this with Telegram, which shares a massive 50% cut of their native ad platform revenue. That might seem perfectly similar, but the actual telegram vs youtube earnings breakdown varies completely differently based on how advertisers purchase space on those respective platforms.

Production Speed and Execution

The sheer overhead of managing a successful YouTube channel is tremendous. High-revenue YouTubers dedicate hours to script writing, filming, pacing edits, and crafting visually flawless thumbnails for just one single upload. Telegram is an entirely different battleground. A creator on Telegram can draft an insightful 200-word text post, attach a quick image from their phone, hit publish, and instantly reach millions of push-notified devices. The content delivery turnaround on Telegram vastly outperforms YouTube’s lengthy production cycle.

Direct Sponsorship Agility

Sponsorships exist everywhere, but they are uniquely fluid on Telegram. Securing a $1,000 brand deal requires recording a 60-second integrated shoutout during a YouTube video—a permanent, hard-coded commitment in your content library. Meanwhile, getting paid that exact same competitive CPM rate on Telegram might just involve publishing an advertiser's templated message for 24-48 hours before deleting it. The "delete-after" culture on Telegram maintains an unbelievably clean feed while bringing in incredibly fast, agile income.

The Final Verdict

YouTube is undeniably unparalleled when it comes to algorithmic longevity and search discovery over months or years. A video from 2018 can still generate AdSense money today. Telegram, however, dominates in immediate impact, low friction output, and direct intimacy with an audience. Highly profitable creators are now realizing that layering these forces produces the best results: using YouTube to attract new eyeballs, and funnelling those massive audiences into a private Telegram channel for daily monetization.
