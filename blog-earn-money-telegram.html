How to Earn Money from Telegram Channel in 2025

By TelegramUniverse Team • 5 min read

If you're managing a growing community and you are wondering how to make money on telegram in 2025, you are in the right place. Telegram has evolved from a simple messaging app into a robust publishing platform where admins are cashing in significantly. The shift towards direct creator payments has made finding a solid path to securing a steady telegram channel income easier than ever.

1. Telegram Native Ads Revenue

The most seamless among all telegram monetization methods is tapping into the Telegram Ad Platform. Telegram splits the advertising revenue with public channel owners (with 1000+ subscribers) on a 50/50 basis. Creators are paid in Toncoin (TON), which can be organically withdrawn or reinvested into promoting their own channels. This method turns your regular views into predictable income. If you want to know how much can you earn from telegram via native ads, you can plug your views into our Revenue Calculator to see estimates based on your channel tier.

2. Direct Sponsorships and Paid Posts

Selling ad spots directly to businesses is the oldest way to earn money from telegram channel followings. If you operate in a high-value niche (like crypto, finance, or business), advertisers will pay a premium to be featured in your feed. You can charge fixed rates per 24 hours of placement or per 1,000 views (CPM).

  • Create a public "Advertise with us" contact bio.
  • Join Telegram ad networks and exchanges like Telega.in.
  • Network directly with channels similar to your size and pitch cross-promotions.

3. Subscription Models and Private Channels

With the introduction of Telegram Stars and integrated bot payments, setting up a paid subscription for an exclusive VIP channel is incredibly lucrative. You can keep your main channel free to gather top-of-funnel traffic, and gently push your most engaged subscribers to a private group where you share premium content, signals, or insider insights.

4. Affiliate Marketing

If you have a trusting audience, affiliate marketing can sometimes surpass native ad revenue. You review or recommend a tool, share your unique link, and earn a commission for every sale. The best niches for this are tech deals, fashion, trading software, and digital courses. Always ensure the products actually deliver value so you do not hurt your channel's integrity.

Bringing It All Together

Most successful creators don't just pick one path. They layer their revenue streams—combining native TON earnings with affiliate links and occasional sponsored posts. Start small, track your engagement metrics, and slowly introduce monetization methods to your audience so they don't feel overwhelmed.
